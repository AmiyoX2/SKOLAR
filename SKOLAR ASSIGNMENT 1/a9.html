The top 3 footballers of the last decade

1. LIONEL MESSI

Lionel Messi, widely regarded as one of the greatest footballers of all time, has left an indelible mark on the world of soccer. Born on June 24, 1987, in Rosario, Argentina, Messi began his career at a young age, joining FC Barcelona’s youth academy, La Masia, when he was just 13 years old. His extraordinary talent was evident early on, and he quickly rose through the ranks to make his first-team debut in 2004 at the age of 17. Over the next 17 years at Barcelona, Messi shattered countless records, becoming the club’s all-time top scorer and leading them to numerous titles, including four UEFA Champions League trophies and ten La Liga titles. His ability to dribble past defenders, his vision, and his precise finishing made him a nightmare for opponents and a joy to watch for fans worldwide.

2. CRISTIANO RONALDO

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, is widely regarded as one of the greatest footballers of all time. His professional career began with Sporting Lisbon, but he gained international fame when he signed with Manchester United in 2003 at just 18 years old. During his time at Manchester United, Ronaldo developed into a formidable forward, winning three Premier League titles, a UEFA Champions League title, and his first Ballon d'Or in 2008.

3. NEYMAR JR.

Neymar Jr., born Neymar da Silva Santos Júnior on February 5, 1992, in Brazil, is a professional footballer renowned for his exceptional skill and creativity. He began his career at Santos FC, where he won the Copa Libertadores in 2011. In 2013, he joined FC Barcelona, forming the famous "MSN" trio with Lionel Messi and Luis Suárez, winning numerous titles, including the UEFA Champions League. In 2017, Neymar transferred to Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) for a record fee of €222 million, where he continues to excel. He has won multiple Ligue 1 titles and played a crucial role in PSG's successes. Internationally, Neymar has been instrumental for Brazil, winning the Copa América in 2019 and the Olympic gold medal in 2016. His dribbling, vision, and scoring ability make him one of the most talented footballers of his generation.
